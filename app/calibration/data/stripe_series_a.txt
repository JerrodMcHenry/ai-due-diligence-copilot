Company: Stripe
Stage: Series A
Industry: Fintech / Developer Infrastructure
Business Model: Transaction-based payments infrastructure

Summary:
Stripe builds payment infrastructure for developers and internet businesses. The product lets companies accept payments online through developer-friendly APIs.

Market:
Online payments is a large and rapidly growing market as more commerce moves online. Existing payment solutions are painful for developers and startups.

Team:
Founders are Patrick Collison and John Collison. Strong technical founders with clear product insight and strong developer orientation.

Product:
Developer-first payments API. Strong usability advantage compared with legacy payment processors. Early product loved by startups and developers.

Traction:
Early adoption among startups and internet businesses. Strong developer word-of-mouth. Specific revenue metrics are not provided.

Financial:
Limited public financial information at Series A stage. Business model has potential for transaction-based revenue scaling with payment volume.

Risks:
Payments is highly competitive and regulated. Incumbents include PayPal and legacy merchant processors. Fraud, compliance, and trust are major execution risks.
