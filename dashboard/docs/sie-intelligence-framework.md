# SIE Intelligence Framework v1.0

## Core Idea

The Startup Intelligence Engine does not score startups against every startup in the world.

SIE scores companies relative to what is expected for their current stage, industry, and business model.

A strong pre-seed company can score higher than a weak Series B company because the score measures stage-adjusted strength.

---

## Primary Scores

### Startup Intelligence Score

Question answered:

How strong is this startup relative to comparable startups?

Comparable means similar:

- Stage
- Industry
- Business model

Future comparison dimensions may include:

- Geography
- Revenue model
- Customer segment
- Funding status

The Startup Intelligence Score is the flagship SIE metric.

---

### Readiness Score

Question answered:

How ready is this startup for its next major milestone?

The milestone depends on stage.

Examples:

- Idea: ready to validate the problem
- Pre-seed: ready to raise pre-seed
- Seed: ready for institutional seed
- Series A: ready for Series A
- Series B: ready to scale
- Series C: ready for IPO, acquisition, or late-stage expansion

Readiness is not universal. It is stage-specific.

---

### Momentum Score

Question answered:

Is this startup improving over time?

Momentum is based on score changes, new evidence, traction updates, document uploads, funding progress, customer validation, and operational improvements.

Momentum helps users understand direction, not just current position.

---

## Stage-Specific Evaluation

Each startup stage has its own evaluation framework.

The same categories may appear across stages, but the expectations are different.

Core categories:

- Market
- Team
- Product
- Traction
- Competition
- Financials

Example:

At pre-seed, revenue may be optional.

At Series A, revenue growth and repeatable GTM motion become critical.

---

## Interpretation

A score should always explain:

- What was measured
- What stage it was measured against
- Why the score was assigned
- What would improve the score
- How the company compares to peers

No score should appear without context.

---

## Product Rule

Every displayed score should eventually include:

- Numeric score
- Stage context
- Peer group
- Score breakdown
- Explanation
- Recommended next action

Example:

Startup Intelligence Score: 87 / 100  
Peer Group: Seed AI SaaS companies  
Percentile: Top 10%  
Why: Strong team, clear market, improving traction  
Next Action: Strengthen customer validation and revenue consistency

---

## Initial Focus

SIE supports multiple stages conceptually, but the first product experience should be strongest for:

- Idea stage
- Pre-seed
- Seed

This matches the likely early user base:

- First-time founders
- Early-stage founders
- Angels
- Pre-seed investors
- Seed investors
- Accelerators

Later stages can be supported over time as the dataset and frameworks mature.
